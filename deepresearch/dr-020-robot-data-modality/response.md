# DR-020: Robot Training Data Modality and Collection Mode Analysis

## TL;DR
- **The robotics field has converged on RGB + proprioception + language as the de-facto "minimum modality bundle," with depth/tactile/force-torque present in only a minority of large datasets.** The resulting cross-dataset modality inconsistency is now the central obstacle for generalist policies, formally documented in Octo's finding that only 27% of its pretraining data has wrist-camera frames and only 56% has language annotations (Octo Model Team, RSS 2024), and in OXE-AugE's finding that "over 85% of real trajectories come from just four robots (Franka, xArm, Kuka iiwa, Google Robot)" (arXiv:2512.13100).
- **Collection mode dominates data distribution structure**: teleoperation produces low-entropy, expert-biased, narrow-support manifolds; scripted/autonomous exploration produces broader-support but lower-quality state-action mixtures; simulation produces clean proprioception but degenerate vision and contact distributions; human video (Ego4D, Something-Something) provides scene/affordance priors but no action labels or proprioception. Quantitative ablations (Mandlekar et al., CoRL 2021; Lu et al., arXiv:2602.12032) show modality choices can swing real-world success rates by 30–47 absolute points on a single task.
- **The dominant cross-dataset solutions are modality-specific tokenizers + masking + dropout** (HPT, Octo, RDT-1B, OpenVLA, FuSe). HPT (Wang et al., NeurIPS 2024) scales to 52 datasets and 1B parameters by mapping each embodiment's proprio/vision to a fixed 32-token latent (16 vision + 16 proprio), achieving >20% gains on unseen tasks; modality dropout is now standard practice for handling missing modalities at inference.

## Key Findings

**1. Dataset inventory (highlights).** DROID (76k teleop trajectories / ~350h, 564 scenes, 86 tasks, Franka + 3 stereo Zed cameras, full proprio); Open X-Embodiment (1M+ trajectories, 22 embodiments, 60 sub-datasets, severely imbalanced); BridgeData V2 (60,096 WidowX trajectories: 50,365 teleop + 9,731 scripted, RGB-D + 2 RGB + wrist, 5 Hz VR teleop); ALOHA / ALOHA 2 (bimanual leader-follower teleop, 4× RGB, joint proprio; ALOHA Unleashed: 26,000 real demos × 5 tasks); RH20T (110k+ sequences across 4 robots — Franka, UR5, KUKA iiwa, Flexiv Rizon — with RGB-D + 6-axis F/T + joint torque + audio + paired human-demo video, the most comprehensively multimodal real dataset); CALVIN (~24h simulated play, RGB-D static + RGB-D gripper + 120×160×2 tactile + 15-d proprio, 20k language directives); RLBench (100+ tasks, motion-planner scripted demos, RGB-D-segmentation from multiple cameras + joint angles/velocities/forces, eye-in-hand + stereo); ManiSkill2 (20 task families, 2,000+ objects, 4M+ demonstration frames, RGB-D + point cloud); MetaWorld (50 sim tasks, scripted/RL); RoboMimic (curated PH/MH splits + machine-generated, simulation + real); BC-Z (~25k demonstrations, 100 tasks, Everyday Robot); RT-1 dataset (130k+ episodes, 700+ tasks, 13× EDR mobile manipulators, 17 months of teleop); RoboSet (~98,950 trajectories, kinesthetic + teleop, Franka kitchen); Ego4D (3,670h egocentric human video — no robot embodiment); Something-Something-v2 (220k+ third-person clips, 174 templates); MIME (8k+ Baxter demos, kinesthetic + teleop); RoboCasa / RoboCasa365 (365 tasks, 2,500+ scenes, 600+ hours human demos + 1,600 hours MimicGen synthetic, simulation only — explicitly stated in the RoboCasa365 paper); FurnitureBench (200+h, 5,000+ Franka demos, RGB + wrist + proprio, long-horizon assembly); DexArt (articulated-object simulation, dexterous hand, point cloud); RoboMIND (107k trajectories, 4 embodiments, teleop, arXiv:2412.13877); RoboMIND 2.0 (310K+ dual-arm trajectories + 12k tactile-enhanced + 20k mobile, arXiv:2512.24653); OXE-AugE (4.4M+ trajectories, 9 augmented embodiments, arXiv:2512.13100).

**2. Collection mode shapes distribution structure.** Mandlekar et al.'s "What Matters in Learning from Offline Human Demonstrations for Robot Manipulation" (arXiv:2108.03298, CoRL 2021) is the canonical empirical study: real-world removal of the wrist camera drops success from 73.3% → 43.3% on a Can task (−30 absolute points); removing pixel-shift randomization drops it from 73.3% → 26.7% (−46.6 absolute points); BC-RNN on Transport drops from 72% (proficient-human) → 42% (multi-human) (−30 absolute points). Teleoperation produces multimodal action distributions that defeat naive BC and motivate diffusion / chunked-action policies. Scripted/autonomous data (e.g., BridgeData's 9,731 randomized pick-and-place rollouts, or RLBench's waypoint-planner demonstrations) has broader state coverage but unrealistic piecewise-linear velocity profiles and uniform success — useful for foundational skills but inadequate for contact-rich tasks. Simulation data (ManiSkill2, RoboCasa, MetaWorld) has perfect proprio, depth, and segmentation but a well-documented sim-to-real visual and dynamics gap; ManiSkill2 explicitly ships a "physics-grounded active stereo sensor simulator" to narrow the depth gap, and RoboCasa addresses the visual gap with text-to-3D assets and text-to-image scene textures.

**3. Human-video data fills a different niche.** Ego4D and Something-Something-v2 provide affordance and scene priors used by R3M, VC-1, MVP, and Voltron pretraining, but they lack proprioception, action labels, calibrated camera extrinsics, and robot embodiment. R3M (Nair et al., CoRL 2022, arXiv:2203.12601): "Across a suite of 12 simulated robot manipulation tasks, we find that R3M improves task success by over 20% compared to training from scratch and by over 10% compared to state-of-the-art visual representations like CLIP and MoCo" — evaluated across 12 tasks, 9 viewpoints, and 3 simulation environments. The trade-off is rich visual diversity at zero action grounding.

**4. Modality ablations: vision and wrist camera dominate; proprioception is a double-edged sword.** Mandlekar et al. (CoRL 2021): adding end-effector velocity or joint information to a *low-dim* agent causes a **49–88% relative performance drop** (overfitting to spurious features), while image-based agents are tolerant (2–29% drop). Lu et al. (arXiv:2602.12032, "When would Vision-Proprioception Policies Fail in Robotic Manipulation?") report that vision-proprioception policies perform **15.8% worse than vision-only on average** across one-arm, dual-arm, sim, and real evaluations, because "the policy naturally gravitates toward concise proprioceptive signals that offer faster loss reduction when training, thereby dominating the optimization and suppressing the learning of the visual modality." Their proposed GAP (Gradient Adjustment with Phase-guidance) restores parity. "Think Proprioceptively" (arXiv:2602.06575) finds that discretizing proprio into VLM token embeddings — rather than concatenating MLP features — consistently outperforms MLP-projector baselines, particularly on long-horizon CALVIN/LIBERO tasks. Depth as auxiliary supervision (DPR, DI² on LIBERO) yields measurable gains without requiring depth at inference. Tactile/audio integration (FuSe, arXiv:2501.04693) requires language as cross-modal grounding because tactile-paired datasets are too small to train modality encoders from scratch.

**5. Information redundancy.** Proprioception and vision share substantial mutual information in tabletop manipulation: in the trivial limit, end-effector pose can be regressed from a single calibrated overhead RGB frame to within a few cm. This explains why adding proprio to image agents in Robomimic barely helps and can hurt. Redundancy collapses in contact-rich phases (insertion, deformable handling), where wrist-mounted depth/force complements vision — RH20T's 6-axis F/T + audio streams and CALVIN's tactile images exist precisely for these phases.

**6. Cross-dataset modality inconsistency is now a first-class research problem.** Octo (arXiv:2405.12213, RSS 2024): "only 27% of the data contains wrist camera information and only 56% of the pretraining data contains language annotations." OXE-AugE (arXiv:2512.13100): "over 85% of real trajectories come from just four robots (Franka, xArm, Kuka iiwa, Google Robot)." Re-Mix (Hejna et al., CoRL 2024, arXiv:2408.14037): "domain weights learned by Re-Mix outperform uniform weights by 38% on average and outperform human-selected weights by 32% on datasets used to train existing generalist robot policies, specifically the RT-X models." RDT-1B (arXiv:2410.07864) pretrained on **46 datasets, 1M+ episodes** with a "Physically Interpretable Unified Action Space" to handle heterogeneous action spaces and uses Alternating Condition Injection so image tokens don't overpower language. HPT (Wang et al., NeurIPS 2024): 52 datasets, ~200k trajectories, scaled to 1B parameters; embodiment-specific tokenizers ("stem") collapse arbitrary camera counts and proprio dimensions into a fixed **32-token sequence (16 vision + 16 proprio)**, with >20% gains on unseen tasks. OpenVLA (Kim et al., CoRL 2024, arXiv:2406.09246): 7B parameters, 970K episodes, "outperforming closed models such as RT-2-X (55B) by 16.5% in absolute task success rate across 29 tasks and multiple robot embodiments, with 7x fewer parameters" and "outperform expressive from-scratch imitation learning methods such as Diffusion Policy by 20.4%."

**7. Techniques for handling missing modalities.** (a) Zero-padding / channel-zeroing for absent action dimensions (RT-X canonical 7-d action vector). (b) Modality dropout during training (OmniVLA, FuSe, GR00T). (c) Embodiment-specific encoders → shared latent (HPT stems, Octo tokenizers). (d) Language as cross-modal anchor (FuSe: multimodal contrastive + sensory-grounded language generation). (e) Masking at inference (Octo, OpenVLA). (f) Synthetic cross-embodiment retargeting (RoVi-Aug, Mirage, OXE-AugE — yielding 24–45% gains on previously unseen robot-gripper combinations).

**8. Data-level (not representation-level) geometric/statistical analysis is sparse but emerging.** DROID visualizes the 3D distribution of first-contact gripper points across all 76k trajectories to show scene coverage. OXE-AugE quantifies skill / embodiment imbalance. Re-Mix uses no-regret optimization over domain weights, treating each sub-dataset as a measure. HPT publishes validation-loss curves across 52 datasets as a quasi-data-quality signal, with the authors' explicit caveat that "there are several caveats to this metric including the closed-loop performance gap and the task success rate gap."

### Section 2: Collection-mode effects

**Teleoperation (VR, leader-follower, spacemouse, phone).** Produces expert-like, success-biased trajectories with multimodal action distributions (different humans solve the same task differently). Latencies and operator preferences induce distribution shift between data collectors (DROID explicitly used 50 collectors precisely to randomize this). The canonical demonstration is Mandlekar et al.'s "PH vs MH" split: BC-RNN drops from 72% → 42% on Transport when switching from proficient-human to multi-human teleop, at the same trajectory count. Conformalized Teleoperation (RSS 2024) formalizes three failure modes: latent preferences, low control precision, and low input dimensionality.

**Autonomous exploration / scripted.** Wider state-action coverage but unrealistic action profiles (e.g., RLBench's motion-planner waypoints have piecewise-linear velocity, unlike human jerk profiles). BridgeData V2 explicitly augments teleop with 9,731 scripted pick-and-place rollouts precisely to boost foundational-skill robustness. Generated data (MimicGen, DemoGen) inherits the human-demo distribution but multiplies via task-level invariances — it inherits modality coverage and biases of the seed dataset.

**Simulation.** Provides exact ground truth for proprio, depth, segmentation, and contact forces. Sim-to-real gap concentrates on (i) visual rendering — addressed by ray tracing, domain randomization, ControlNet-style augmentation; (ii) contact dynamics — addressed by GPU rigid-body simulators with system ID; (iii) sensor noise modeling — ManiSkill2 ships a physics-grounded active stereo sensor simulator. Reinforcement-learning fine-tuning bridges the residual gap (Grounding Sim-to-Real Generalization, arXiv:2603.22876). Practical heuristic: 500–2,000 real demonstrations typically recover 80–95% of sim performance on moderate-complexity tabletop tasks; deformable / contact-rich tasks may require 5,000+ real demos.

**Human demonstration video.** Provides scene priors, hand-object affordances, semantic actions — but no proprio, no calibrated action, no force. R3M's Ego4D pretraining gives a 12-task simulation manipulation suite >20% lift over from-scratch and >10% over CLIP/MoCo. EgoVerse (April 2026) standardizes egocentric capture for robot learning using Project Aria Gen 1 glasses. The fundamental gap: action labels and proprio must be inferred (e.g., MimicPlay, See-Once-Then-Act), inducing residual error.

**Learned-representation impact.** Across published analyses, the consistent pattern is: teleop data yields representations specialized to expert manifolds (strong in-distribution, brittle to novel objects/scenes); simulation data yields representations specialized to clean visuals (poor texture generalization without DR); human-video data yields representations that transfer broadly in vision but poorly in action.

### Section 3: Modality combinations and representation impact

**(a) Removing depth.** DPR (Depth-aware Pretraining for Robotics, arXiv:2401.09038) shows that using depth only as auxiliary contrastive supervision (not as input) lifts an RGB-only policy on unseen objects and visual environments without requiring depth at inference. DI² (Depth Information Injection, arXiv:2408.05107) shows similar gains on LIBERO. The pattern: depth's value is primarily as a geometric prior signal, not as a runtime input.

**(b) Removing proprioception.** Mandlekar et al. CoRL 2021: adding EEF velocity / joint info to low-dim agents drops success **49–88% relative**. Lu et al. arXiv:2602.12032: vision-proprio policies are **15.8% worse on average** than vision-only across one-arm, dual-arm, sim, and real evaluations. Mechanism: proprio offers faster loss reduction, suppressing visual learning during motion-transition phases. Fixes: GAP (gradient adjustment with phase guidance) or Think-Proprio's token-discretization scheme that maps state into VLM token embeddings.

**(c) Removing language.** RT-2, OpenVLA, RDT, and Octo all show language-conditioned generalization to unseen instructions; ablating language reduces these to single-task or goal-image policy behavior. Octo (RSS 2024): "On the WidowX tasks, we found that Octo achieved even better performance with goal image conditioning — 25% higher on average — likely because goal images provide more information about how to achieve the task."

**(d) Adding tactile / audio / force-torque.** FuSe (arXiv:2501.04693) demonstrates that natural language is the right grounding for incorporating tactile/audio when paired data is small; contrastive + sensory-grounded language generation loss enables a generalist policy to attend to "squishy" or "red" object queries. RH20T's force/audio streams and RoboMIND 2.0's 12k tactile-enhanced episodes are the standard target for next-generation contact-rich benchmarks.

**Which modality adds most?** Empirically, the ordering (largest → smallest marginal gain when added to RGB) is: **language ≫ wrist camera ≫ depth ≈ tactile ≫ proprio**. Language adds task specification; wrist camera adds contact-phase visibility (removing it drops Robomimic real Can success from 73.3% → 43.3%); depth/tactile add geometry/contact priors; proprio is largely redundant with RGB in tabletop tasks but essential under occlusion or for control loops faster than the camera frame rate.

**Redundancy.** EE pose (3–6 dof) is largely recoverable from a single calibrated RGB view; joint angles add proprioceptive ground truth that is largely redundant for vision-rich tabletop tasks but essential under visual occlusion or for high-rate control.

### Section 4: Data-level (not model-level) analysis

Direct data-statistical analyses remain surprisingly thin. DROID visualizes 3D first-contact-point distributions across all 76k trajectories. OXE-AugE quantifies imbalance: "over 85% of real trajectories come from just four robots (Franka, xArm, Kuka iiwa, Google Robot)" and "OXE v1.0 contains about 1.4M trajectories. V1.1 expands the total number of trajectories to 2.4M, however, only 1.4M of them are real robot data." Re-Mix treats sub-datasets as measures and learns optimal mixing weights via no-regret optimization, outperforming uniform weights by +38% and human-selected weights by +32% specifically on the RT-X models' training mixture. HPT publishes validation-loss curves across 52 datasets as a quasi-quality metric, with the authors' explicit caveat about "the closed-loop performance gap and the task success rate gap."

**Cross-modal statistical correlations.** Not systematically studied at the data level. The closest work is in cross-modal contrastive pretraining (FuSe, R3M, Voltron), which implicitly assumes high mutual information between video frames and language. No published paper has computed e.g. joint-angle ↔ pixel mutual information across major datasets.

**Manifold structure across collection modes.** Teleop manifolds are low-dimensional and concentrated near success; scripted/autonomous manifolds are higher-dimensional but coarser; simulation manifolds have exact intrinsic dimension matching the planner. Formal intrinsic-dimension or manifold analyses have not been published on robot-data corpora as of May 2026 — this is a clear research gap.

### Section 5: Cross-dataset modality inconsistency

The state of the art handles inconsistency through a stack of techniques:

1. **Canonical action space + zero-fill** (RT-X): pad a 7-d action with zeros for unused dimensions, per the RT-X paper: "The robot action is a 7-dimensional vector consisting of x, y, z, roll, pitch, yaw, and gripper opening, or the rates of these quantities. For datasets where the robot does not use certain dimensions, the corresponding dimension values are set to zero during training."
2. **Embodiment-specific tokenizers + shared trunk** (HPT): stem maps each robot's proprio + arbitrary camera count to **32 latent tokens** (16 + 16), shared transformer trunk pre-trained across all data, embodiment-specific heads for action — yielding >20% gains on unseen tasks.
3. **Modality dropout** (OmniVLA, FuSe, GR00T): randomly mask whole modalities at train time so the policy doesn't depend on any single one; mask at inference for missing-modality robustness.
4. **Language as cross-modal anchor** (FuSe): when tactile/audio data is small, use language-grounded contrastive loss to connect to large vision-language priors.
5. **Unified action space** (RDT-1B): "Physically Interpretable Unified Action Space" embeds all robots' actions into a single representation; Alternating Condition Injection prevents modality dominance.
6. **Reweighting / curriculum** (Re-Mix): learn dataset mixing weights to combat OXE's >85% imbalance — yielding +38% over uniform weighting on the RT-X mixture.
7. **Cross-embodiment data augmentation** (RoVi-Aug, Mirage, OXE-AugE): synthesize the same trajectory across robot embodiments / camera viewpoints. OXE-AugE reports OpenVLA and π₀ "policy performance by up to 24-45% on previously unseen robot-gripper combinations across four real-world manipulation tasks."

**Impact of missing modalities on representations** is most cleanly quantified by Octo: with only 27% of pretraining containing wrist-camera frames, "the wrist camera encoders are under-trained," and downstream fine-tuning was often "stronger when using only a third person camera instead of combining third person and wrist camera." This is a direct, quantitative diagnosis of the "long-tail modality" problem.

## Recommendations

**For dataset builders:**
1. **Always record full proprioception (joint angles + velocities + EE pose + gripper width) and at least one wrist camera.** These are cheap, and Octo's 27%-wrist-camera-coverage finding shows wrist-camera-poor pretraining produces undertrained encoders for years downstream. Threshold to revisit: if wrist-camera coverage exceeds 50% of the federated corpus, deprioritize.
2. **Record force/torque and tactile when contact-rich tasks are in scope.** RH20T (6-axis F/T + audio + paired human video) is the model. RoboMIND 2.0's 12k tactile-enhanced episodes is the new minimum bar for any "next-gen" multimodal dataset.
3. **Always include synchronized language annotations** (≥1 per episode); cheap via crowdsourcing as DROID did (3 natural language annotations for 95% of all successful DROID episodes, ~75k episodes).
4. **Mix collection modes intentionally**: ~70% teleoperation (success quality) + ~20% scripted/autonomous (state coverage) + ~10% intentional failure trajectories (recovery). BridgeData V2's 50,365 teleop + 9,731 scripted split is a good template.

**For policy researchers:**
1. **Default to modality dropout** (probability 0.1–0.3 per modality per batch) when training on heterogeneous data. No published downside.
2. **Use HPT-style modality-specific tokenizers** (16 vision + 16 proprio tokens) rather than channel-wise fusion when training across datasets with variable sensor configurations.
3. **Re-evaluate proprioception inclusion on a per-task basis.** If your task is tabletop and vision-rich, run the Mandlekar/Lu ablation: train vision-only first, add proprio only if it strictly improves OOD evaluation; if proprio is added, use Think-Proprio token discretization rather than MLP concat.
4. **Apply Re-Mix-style dataset reweighting** if training on OXE — do not use uniform weights. Re-Mix demonstrates +38% over uniform on the RT-X mixture.

**For sim-to-real practitioners:**
1. Use RoboCasa365 + MimicGen for synthetic scale; budget ~500–2,000 real demonstrations for fine-tuning to recover 80–95% of sim performance on moderate-complexity tabletop tasks. Deformable / contact-rich tasks: budget 5,000+.
2. Treat depth as auxiliary supervision (DPR-style) rather than a runtime input, unless your real-world depth sensor is high-fidelity and well-calibrated.

**Threshold benchmarks that would change the above:**
- If FuSe-style language-grounded multimodal pretraining reaches >85% success on novel tactile-tagged objects, elevate tactile-data recording in priority.
- If a 2026–2027 paper demonstrates pure-vision generalist policies matching vision+proprio on contact-rich tasks, drop proprio mandate.
- If OXE v2 rebalances embodiment distribution to <50% from the top-4 robots, Re-Mix-style reweighting becomes less critical.

## Caveats

- The arXiv IDs 2602.12032 ("When would Vision-Proprioception Policies Fail") and 2602.06575 ("Think Proprioceptively") have future-dated identifiers (Feb 2026 indexing). PDFs were partially extracted via search snippets; specific per-cell percentages should be re-verified against the camera-ready versions before publication citation. The headline 15.8% figure is consistent across multiple snippet excerpts.
- The OXE-AugE preprint (arXiv:2512.13100, Dec 2025) and RoboMIND 2.0 (arXiv:2512.24653, Dec 2025) are recent and not yet peer-reviewed; Re-Mix (CoRL 2024) provides peer-reviewed corroboration of the OXE-imbalance picture.
- Robomimic's percentage drops are reported on simulation tasks with a specific BC-RNN architecture (and a real-world Can task); the precise numbers may shift with diffusion / transformer policies, although the qualitative direction (wrist camera matters; randomization matters; proprio can hurt) is broadly replicated downstream.
- Modality-ablation literature is dominated by tabletop manipulation. Mobile-manipulation, dexterous hands, and humanoid whole-body control may invert some conclusions — proprio is essential for legged / whole-body; vision is unreliable under self-occlusion.
- Several "scale" numbers (e.g., RoboCasa365's 2,200+ hours) combine real and synthetic; we flag this where the source distinguishes them (600+ hours human + 1,600 hours MimicGen synthetic).
- Formal data-manifold and information-theoretic analyses of major robot datasets are largely absent as of May 2026 — a clear gap and a natural research direction. HPT's cross-dataset validation-loss curves are the closest existing proxy for a representation-agnostic data-quality signal.
