# Representation Analysis as a Standard Tool in Robot Policy Training (DR-022)

## TL;DR
- Representation analysis has already paid off in robotics in five concrete, published cases — **ReVLA** (depth-probe diagnosis → +77% OOD grasping), **D²PPO** (clustering-based collapse diagnosis → dispersive loss, +22.7%/+26.1% on RoboMimic), **RS-CL** (CKNNA alignment diagnosis → contrastive loss, 30.8% → 41.5% on RoboCasa pick-and-place), **Lei et al. 2026 "Science of Co-Training"** (Wasserstein-based structured-representation-alignment diagnosis → CFG-ADDA, ~21/30 average success vs. 15.3/30 vanilla), and **Dr. VLA / Häon et al.** (SAE + FFN-projection diagnosis → activation steering and memorization detection). These are existence proofs that *post-hoc representation analysis can drive concrete training fixes*, but they remain bespoke one-off pipelines.
- The biggest unexploited opportunities are (i) using CKA/Procrustes as an *online* distillation loss for VLAs (mature in CV/NLP — e.g., Zhou et al. "Rethinking Centered Kernel Alignment in Knowledge Distillation," IJCAI 2024, pp. 5680–5688 — but absent in robotics), (ii) running a *probe suite as a continuous training callback* analogous to loss curves, and (iii) using SAE memorization-vs-generalization classifiers to gate data ingestion — none of these is yet standard practice in any robotics training pipeline.
- A "Representation Analysis Toolkit for Robot Training" should be built as a thin robotics-specific adapter over **SAE-Lens + TransformerLens + netrep + repsim**, plus a robotics-native probe registry (proprioception, gripper-state, depth, language-grounding, success-prediction). Recommendation: invest in the adapter layer first (~1 engineer-month) rather than rebuilding probes/SAE infrastructure.

## Key Findings

### 1. Representation-analysis-driven training improvements that already exist

| Case | Diagnostic tool | Problem found | Fix | Reported gain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ReVLA** (Dey et al., arXiv 2409.15250) | Linear probe + DPT head for depth regression on DINOv2 features | Catastrophic forgetting in OpenVLA's DINOv2 backbone — "the trained vision encoder in OpenVLA collapses to a constant output for DPT and generates depth maps without details for linear probing" | Gradual backbone reversal via model merging | +77% (grasping) and +66% (lifting) relative on visual OOD SIMPLER tasks |
| **D²PPO** (Zou et al., arXiv 2508.02644) | Clustering analysis of hidden-layer features (algorithm unnamed in paper) | Diffusion representation collapse: "numerous similar state representations are grouped into nearly identical clusters" | Dispersive loss (in-batch InfoNCE-L2, InfoNCE-Cosine, or Hinge variants) regularizing intermediate features | +22.7% pretraining / +26.1% fine-tune avg. on RoboMimic |
| **RS-CL** (Kim et al., arXiv 2510.01711) | CKNNA (Centered Kernel Nearest-Neighbor Alignment, Huh et al. 2024 "Platonic Representation Hypothesis") between VLM hidden states and proprioception | VLM representations not sensitive to robotic signals — "the VLM lacks the capability to capture robotic signals" | Soft-supervised contrastive loss weighted by proprioceptive-state distance + view-cutoff augmentation | 30.8% → 41.5% (RoboCasa pick-place); 45.0% → 58.3% (real robot) |
| **Lei et al. 2026** (arXiv 2604.13645, "Science of Co-Training") | UMAP visualization + Wasserstein distance between sim/real latents + 2-layer MLP domain classifier | Co-training only works when representations are "structured aligned" — close but not collapsed; physics-only gaps cause negative correlation | CFG-ADDA (classifier-free guidance + adversarial discriminative domain adaptation) | 21/30 avg real-world success vs. 15.3/30 vanilla co-training across three tasks (~37% relative improvement) |
| **Dr. VLA / Swann et al.** (arXiv 2603.19183) | Sparse Autoencoders on residual stream of π0.5 and OpenVLA; logistic regression on four temporal feature statistics (onset count, episode coverage, mean activation magnitude, relative run length) to classify general-vs-memorized features | Most SAE features encode memorized trajectory snippets, not generalizable concepts; small-scale fine-tuning amplifies memorization | Recommend larger/more diverse training data or knowledge insulation; enables feature steering | Open-sourced toolkit announced (code "coming soon" at drvla.github.io) |
| **Häon et al. | **Moalla et al.** (arXiv 2405.00662, "No Representation, No Trust") | Feature-rank and capacity-loss measurement during PPO | Representation collapse precedes performance collapse in on-policy RL; coupled with trust-region degradation | Diagnostic — motivates representation-aware PPO variants | Causal link demonstrated on Atari and MuJoCo |
| **Cheng et al. 2025** "Generalizable DA for sim-and-real" (arXiv 2509.18631) | Optimal-Transport-based feature regularization + UMAP of latents | Modality gap between sim and real, exacerbated by imbalanced mini-batches | Unbalanced OT + temporally-aware sampling | Up to +30% avg success in sim-to-real |

#### Analogous CV/NLP cases ready for transfer
- **CKA-based distillation** is mature in CV/NLP: Zhou et al. "Rethinking Centered Kernel Alignment in Knowledge Distillation," IJCAI 2024 Main Track, pp. 5680–5688 (doi:10.24963/ijcai.2024/628) derives RCKA and PCKA frameworks that decouple CKA into an MMD upper bound and achieve SOTA on CIFAR-100, ImageNet-1k, MS-COCO. CKA-ViT (Gla AccML 2024) achieves 4–8× model size reduction with minimal accuracy loss. **No published work yet applies CKA as a distillation loss between a teacher VLA and a student policy.**
- **Probe-based training diagnostics** are mature in NLP (Hewitt & Manning structural probes; Limisiewicz & Mareček orthogonal-constrained probes for BERT/ELMo; LPASS layer-probe-accuracy-driven pruning of LLMs; DEPPROBE for labeled dependency trees) and recently in DQNs (DuPlessie MIT-PRIMES 2024 SAEs on Deep Q-Networks). **In robotics, probes are used post-hoc (e.g., Lu et al. linear probes on OpenVLA — "probe accuracies exceed 90% for most layers") but not yet as continuous training-time signals.**
- **Modality-gap analysis** (Weixin Liang, Yuhui Zhang, Yongchan Kwon, Serena Yeung, James Zou, "Mind the Gap: Understanding the Modality Gap in Multi-modal Contrastive Representation Learning," NeurIPS 2022, Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 35:17612–17625, arXiv:2203.02053) and contrastive-gap closure (Eslami & de Melo 2024 "It's Not a Modality Gap") are well-developed for CLIP-family models; **no VLA paper has yet used modality-gap metrics to choose fusion strategies**.
- **Intrinsic-dimension / manifold-capacity tracking during training** is established theoretically (Jialin Mao, Itay Griniasty, Han Kheng Teoh, Rahul Ramesh, Rubing Yang, Mark K. Transtrum, James P. Sethna, Pratik Chaudhari, "The training process of many deep networks explores the same low-dimensional manifold," PNAS 121(12):e2310002121, March 12, 2024, doi:10.1073/pnas.2310002121; Birdal et al. "Intrinsic Dimension, Persistent Homology and Generalization in Neural Networks") but **not yet used in robot-policy curricula or early stopping**.

### 2. Lei et al. 2026 — Structured Representation Alignment (SRA) and CFG-ADDA

From the project page (science-of-co-training.github.io) and arXiv 2604.13645 (Yu Lei, Minghuan Liu, Abhiram Maddukuri, Zhenyu Jiang, Yuke Zhu, UT Austin + Amazon FAR + NVIDIA, posted April 15, 2026):

**SRA, formally defined**: when training a co-trained diffusion policy with encoder $f_\phi: \mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{Z}$ and policy $\pi_\theta: \mathcal{Z} \to \mathcal{A}$, the optimal score function decomposes as $s^*_w = \hat w_t s^*_t + \hat w_s s^*_s$ with a kernel-weighted dynamic ratio $\hat w_t = wp_t / (wp_t + (1-w)p_s)$. SRA is the *property* that source and target latents are simultaneously (a) **aligned** — close in $\mathcal{Z}$ so source neighbors contribute useful gradients to target predictions — and (b) **discernible** — separable enough that a domain classifier can still recover the source/target label (in practice, a 2-layer MLP recovers domain at ~100% accuracy even when UMAP shows alignment). Three regimes emerge: **disjoint** (no transfer), **structured-aligned** (the desirable regime), and **overlapping** (full collapse → bimodal action predictions, negative transfer).

**Measurement**: alignment is measured with Wasserstein distance between source and target latent distributions; discernibility with a small MLP domain classifier on the same latents. The paper reports Pearson/Spearman correlations of ~0.6–0.8 between Wasserstein alignment and task success rate. SRA explains ~50% of loss variance; the importance-reweighting effect (modulated by the mixing ratio $w$) explains ~20%.

**CFG-ADDA**: a unification of two prior techniques. **ADDA** (Tzeng et al. 2017 Adversarial Discriminative Domain Adaptation, instantiated for robots by Yuan et al. 2025 MotionTrans) injects an adversarial discriminator that promotes alignment. **CFG** (Classifier-Free Guidance applied to co-training; Wei et al. IROS 2025 "Empirical Analysis of Sim-and-Real Cotraining of Diffusion Policies for Planar Pushing from Pixels") conditions the diffusion model on a domain label, preserving discernibility. CFG-ADDA runs both jointly: the ADDA loss aligns the latents while CFG conditioning preserves domain identity for inference-time control. The paper reports **21/30 average success across three tasks (NutAssembly, MugCleanup, MugHang) vs. 15.3/30 for vanilla co-training and 14.3/30 for OT or ADDA alone**, with the strongest improvement on NutAssembly (23/30 at w=0.016).

**Generalization beyond sim-real**: the project page explicitly lists "sim-and-real, cross-embodiment, cross-modality" as data-source types whose mixing CFG-ADDA addresses. The theoretical framework is agnostic to the source domain — it just requires (a) a mixing ratio $w$, (b) a domain label, and (c) latents whose alignment can be measured. The paper provides a closed-form mixing-ratio bound: $w_n = N/(N+M)$ (natural lower bound) and $w_q = \sqrt{N/M}$ when $M/N > 5$, otherwise $w_q = Nq/((1-q)M + Nq)$ for a desired target-contribution $q$. This generalizes verbatim to cross-embodiment ($N$ = target-robot data, $M$ = cross-embodiment data) and cross-modality (e.g., human video as $M$). The authors also note SRA can be learned implicitly with balanced $w$ — local geometric alignment emerges in shallow layers and global alignment in deep layers without an explicit loss.

### 3. Practical, toolizable methods

Ranked by *(low compute, no extra labels, online-compatible)*:

| Method | Cost per step | Extra labels needed | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Linear probe accuracy** on cached activations | Cheap — solve a least-squares per checkpoint; <1% training overhead if run every ~1k steps | Yes, but usually free (proprioception, gripper state, success/failure, depth from sim) | Real-time training-callback diagnostic (analogous to validation loss) |
| **CKA / CKNNA / Procrustes** between checkpoints or between domains | O(B²) for batch B; trivially cheap for B≤256 | None | Checkpoint selection, domain alignment diagnosis, distillation loss |
| **Wasserstein distance** between sim/real latents | O(B² log B); cheap with mini-batches | Domain label | Co-training diagnostic (Lei et al. 2026) |
| **Intrinsic-dimension** (TwoNN, MLE estimators) | O(B log B); cheap | None | Early stopping signal, curriculum trigger |
| **Modality-gap norm** (mean-distance between modality centroids) | O(B); trivially cheap | Modality labels (free) | Architecture selection (e.g., when to insert cross-attention) |
| **SAE training** | Expensive — separate training run, hours-days on a single VLA layer | None | Post-training diagnostics only (not online) |
| **2-layer MLP domain classifier** | Cheap; train alongside policy | Domain label | Discernibility check (Lei et al. 2026) |
| **Feature-rank / effective dimension** (Moalla 2024) | Cheap (SVD of activation matrix) | None | PPO collapse early-warning |

The big toolkit-design implication: **the first four methods can all be wrapped in a single `on_step_end` callback that consumes the activation cache from a forward hook**. SAE is the outlier — it must run as a separate offline step.

### 4. Open research directions (highest expected value)

1. **CKA / Procrustes as a VLA distillation loss.** Mature in CV (Zhou et al. PCKA, IJCAI 2024) and quantization (CKA-ViT, AccML 2024). Robotics application would compress large generalist VLAs (π0.5, GR00T-N1, RT-2-X) into small task-specific policies while preserving structural representational content. *Nobody has published this for robotics yet.*
2. **Continuous probe suite as a training callback.** A canonical robotics probe set: gripper-open/close classifier, end-effector-pose regressor, depth regressor, language-instruction-recovery probe, success-prediction probe. Logged per epoch and overlaid on the loss curve. Would catch ReVLA-style catastrophic forgetting *during* training rather than only post-hoc.
3. **SAE memorization-vs-generalization gate on data ingestion.** The Dr. VLA logistic-regression classifier (onset count, episode coverage, magnitude, run length → general vs memorized) could be inverted: if a candidate training episode would primarily activate "memorized" features in the current policy, downweight or filter it. This is data attribution by representational signature, distinct from gradient-based attribution.
4. **Modality-gap-guided fusion architecture search.** Use the Liang et al. NeurIPS 2022 modality-gap metric on a small candidate set of fusion designs (early concat, late cross-attention, perceiver-style) and pick the one with the smallest gap conditional on downstream task performance. Currently fusion architecture is selected by sweeping seeds.
5. **Cross-embodiment alignment analysis to set data mixing ratios.** Lei et al.'s mixing-ratio formula ($w_n = N/(N+M)$, $w_q = \sqrt{N/M}$) is currently tested only in sim-real; applying it to Open-X / cross-embodiment with explicit CKA-based "embodiment gap" measurement is an obvious next step that has not been published.
6. **Intrinsic-dimension early-stopping.** Existing CV results (Mao, Griniasty, Teoh, Ramesh, Yang, Transtrum, Sethna, Chaudhari, PNAS 2024) show training explores a shared low-dimensional manifold; in robotics, ID monotone collapse correlates with capacity loss (Moalla 2024). A simple ID-plateau detector could replace ad-hoc step-count budgets.
7. **Structured Representation Alignment for cross-modality.** Lei et al. mention but do not test cross-modality (human video, VLM commonsense). CFG-ADDA's mechanism does not depend on the data being from a simulator; testing it on video-only co-training is a clean follow-up.

### 5. Representation Analysis Toolkit for Robot Training — design

**Target users**: VLA / diffusion-policy / RL practitioners running training jobs on Open-X, DROID, RoboCasa, LIBERO, or proprietary datasets, who want representation diagnostics without writing custom hook code each time.

**What already exists and is reusable**
- **TransformerLens v3.2.1** (TransformerLensOrg/TransformerLens, released May 9, 2026 per PyPI; 3.1k GitHub stars as of May 2026). The v3 TransformerBridge layer increased model support from ~200 to ~9,000 models across 50+ architectures, providing hook points for HuggingFace and JAX models without weight-processing overhead. Reusable as-is for the LLM/VLM backbone of VLAs (PaliGemma, Llama-2 in OpenVLA, Gemma-3, π0.5).
- **SAE-Lens** (github.com/jbloomAus/SAELens, now redirecting to decoderesearch/SAELens; canonical citation: Bloom, Tigges, Duong, Chanin, "SAELens," 2024; 1.4k GitHub stars as of May 2026). SAE training, evaluation (L0, explained variance, reconstruction loss), Neuronpedia export. Reusable directly — Dr. VLA already does this for VLAs.
- **netrep** (ahwillia/netrep): generalized shape metrics (Procrustes, LinearMetric with regularization α). sklearn-style API. Companion paper: Williams, Kunz, Kornblith, Linderman, "Generalized Shape Metrics on Neural Representations," NeurIPS 2021.
- **repsim** (wrongu/repsim): PyTorch-differentiable CKA, AngularCKA, Stress; geodesics between representations. Differentiable outputs make it usable as a loss, not just a metric.
- **rsatoolbox** (van den Bosch et al., bioRxiv 2025) / **NeuroRA** (Frontiers in Neuroinformatics 2020): more mature but neuroscience-oriented; usable for offline analysis.
- **nninfo** (Priesemann-Group/nninfo; 6 GitHub stars confirmed via Releases page; companion to Ehrlich, Schneider, Priesemann, Wibral, Makkeh, "A Measure of the Complexity of Neural Representations based on Partial Information Decomposition," TMLR 2023, arXiv:2209.10438): partial-information-decomposition tools, useful for information-theoretic probes; small and niche.
- **NeuroX** (Dalvi et al., AAAI 2019): neuron-level analysis (originally for NLP, transferable).

**What's missing and must be built**
- A **robotics-native probe registry**: pre-defined probes for gripper state, EE pose, depth, success, language-grounding. Should accept an arbitrary VLA forward function and a dataset, return per-layer probe accuracy curves.
- A **training-time callback API** (Lightning/HuggingFace-Trainer/standalone) that consumes a forward hook every N steps, computes a configurable subset of (probe, CKA, ID, modality-gap, Wasserstein) metrics, and logs to W&B/TensorBoard.
- **Robotics-specific dataloaders / adapters** for activation capture from action experts (diffusion heads, flow-matching heads) — TransformerLens doesn't natively know about diffusion timesteps.
- **An SRA / CFG-ADDA module** implementing Lei et al. 2026's analysis (Wasserstein-on-latents + domain classifier + mixing-ratio guideline).
- **A memorization-vs-generalization classifier** generalizing Dr. VLA's four-feature logistic regression to arbitrary models and datasets.
- **Standard reporting templates**: "representation health report" auto-generated at end of training (analogous to wandb sweep dashboards).

**Recommendation**: build this as a thin top layer (~2-3k lines of Python) named, e.g., `repview` or `policyscope`, that imports SAE-Lens, TransformerLens, netrep, and repsim and exposes a single `RepresentationCallback` class plus a CLI that reads a YAML spec ("which probes, which layers, which metrics, which frequency"). Resist building from scratch — most of the heavy lifting is already done in the LLM-interpretability stack.

## Details

**On the ReVLA probe protocol.** ReVLA uses *two* probes side by side: a linear probe on frozen DINOv2 patch features and a DPT (Dense Prediction Transformer, Ranftl et al.) head. The diagnostic finding — verbatim from the paper: "When tasked with regressing to depth information, the trained vision encoder in OpenVLA collapses to a constant output for DPT and generates depth maps without details for linear probing, reflecting a loss of spatial understanding, while the pretrained DINO-v2 features generate high-quality depth maps, especially with DPT. This collapse is a clear indicator of catastrophic forgetting" — is presented qualitatively in Figure 2, not as a numerical RMSE or δ₁ metric. The headline 77% / 66% improvements are *relative factors* on out-of-domain grasping and lifting in the SIMPLER OOD evaluation framework, not absolute percentage points; the abstract states "improve over OpenVLA by a factor of 77% and 66% for grasping and lifting in visual OOD tasks."

**On D²PPO's "clustering analysis"**: the paper asserts representation collapse via Figure 1(b), described as "clustering analysis of hidden layer features … numerous similar state representations are grouped into nearly identical clusters." The paper does *not* name the specific algorithm (UMAP, t-SNE, k-means, PCA). The remediation — dispersive loss treating all in-batch hidden representations as negative pairs, with three variants (InfoNCE-L2, InfoNCE-Cosine, Hinge) — is a clean fix, but the diagnostic methodology is informal; a more rigorous toolkit would supplement Figure 1(b) with quantitative metrics (silhouette, Davies-Bouldin, effective rank). Code is available at github.com/Guowei-Zou/d2ppo-release.

**On RS-CL's measurement of the modality gap**: rather than running its own discovery experiment, RS-CL invokes CKNNA from Huh et al. 2024 ("Platonic Representation Hypothesis") and finds that pre-RS-CL CKNNA between VLM embeddings and proprioceptive features is low; after RS-CL, "RS-CL increases representation similarity between learned embeddings and proprioceptive features, indicating that RS-CL successfully reshapes the embedding space toward capturing control-relevant signals." CKNNA is used both as the diagnostic and the post-hoc verification metric.

**On Dr. VLA's open-source status**: the project page (drvla.github.io) lists the code as "coming soon" as of the latest snapshot in early 2026; the paper announces release of "an open-source codebase for activation collection, SAE training, and feature steering in VLA models" with three modules and a logistic-regression general-vs-memorized classifier based on four temporal feature statistics. The Häon et al. CoRL 2025 codebase (github.com/vla-mech-interp/mechanistic-steering-vlas, linked from vla-mech-interp.github.io) is available and provides PyTorch FFN hooks for OpenVLA and a modified JAX FFN for π0/π0-FAST, but exposes only FFN-projection + cluster-injection steering, not a general probe/CKA/SAE suite.

**On the "Science of Co-Training" mixing-ratio guideline**: the closed-form recommendation is to search within $(w_n, w_q)$ where $w_n = N/(N+M)$ is the natural ratio and $w_q$ depends on whether $M/N$ exceeds 5. Practitioners can use this *without* any representation analysis tooling, but the paper's framework is most powerful when paired with online Wasserstein-distance and discernibility checks during the sweep. The Cheng et al. 2025 sim-and-real paper (arXiv 2509.18631), which uses unbalanced optimal transport plus temporally-aware sampling, reports up to 30% average success-rate improvement and is one of the three baselines benchmarked in Lei et al. 2026.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 (next 1–2 months)**: build a `RepresentationCallback` that adds three diagnostics to existing OpenVLA / π0 / Diffusion Policy training scripts — (a) linear probes for gripper state, EE pose, and success prediction, run every 1k steps; (b) CKA between current checkpoint and a frozen reference (e.g., pre-fine-tuning DINOv2) to catch ReVLA-style forgetting; (c) feature rank / effective dimension to catch Moalla-style PPO collapse. Wrap netrep / repsim / scikit-learn. Threshold: if probe accuracy drops >10% relative or CKA-to-reference drops below 0.6, fire an alert. Less than 1 engineer-month.

**Stage 2 (2–6 months)**: integrate SAE-Lens for post-training SAE analysis and port Dr. VLA's memorization classifier when it becomes public; add an SRA module (Wasserstein + domain classifier) and the Lei et al. mixing-ratio sweep. Threshold to graduate to Stage 3: at least one published case where the callback caught a problem early enough to save training compute.

**Stage 3 (6–12 months)**: implement CKA-based distillation loss for VLA compression (using repsim's differentiable CKA) and SAE-feature-based data filtering for fine-tuning runs. Threshold: demonstrate ≥10% absolute improvement on at least one Open-X subset by using SAE-feature gating to filter training data, or ≥3× compression with <5% performance drop using CKA-distillation.

**Decision rules**:
- If your training run uses pretrained vision encoders (DINOv2, SigLIP, CLIP), **always run a CKA-to-original-weights probe** every checkpoint — this is the cheapest catastrophic-forgetting detector available, and ReVLA's failure mode is widespread.
- If you co-train on sim+real or human-video+robot data, **always sweep mixing ratios within Lei et al.'s $(w_n, w_q)$ range** and log latent-Wasserstein alongside success rate — explains ~50% of variance for free.
- If you fine-tune a VLA on <500 demos per task, **run Dr. VLA's memorization classifier on the result before deployment** — small-scale fine-tuning disproportionately amplifies memorization features (per the Dr. VLA abstract: "supervised fine-tuning on small robotics datasets disproportionately amplifies memorization").
- Do *not* invest in custom representation-analysis infrastructure before exhausting SAE-Lens + TransformerLens + netrep + repsim; the marginal value of building bespoke tooling is low.

## Caveats

- **Lei et al. 2026 (arXiv 2604.13645) is a preprint dated April 15, 2026** under the "Science of Co-Training" project (Yu Lei, Minghuan Liu, Abhiram Maddukuri, Zhenyu Jiang, Yuke Zhu; UT Austin + Amazon FAR + NVIDIA). Numerical results are the authors' own; the CFG-ADDA improvement (21/30 best-mix vs 15.3/30 vanilla) is from 15 trials × 3 tasks × 3 mixing ratios — moderate sample size, single hardware setup. Reproduction by a second lab is not yet available.
- **ReVLA's 77% / 66% gains are relative factors**, not absolute percentage points. The abstract's phrasing — "improve over OpenVLA by a factor of 77% and 66%" — is ambiguous in some secondary retellings.
- **D²PPO's discovery method is informal** (Figure 1(b) "clustering analysis" with unspecified algorithm). The performance gains (+22.7% / +26.1% on RoboMimic) are real, but the diagnostic step is not yet a reproducible methodology — a notable methodological weakness for citing D²PPO as proof that clustering tools can systematically diagnose diffusion representation collapse.
- **Dr. VLA's code is announced but not yet released** as of the latest available project-page snapshot. Plans to depend on it should include a fallback (e.g., implementing the four-statistic logistic regression manually over SAE-Lens features).
- **Most CKA / probe / SAE infrastructure was built for LLMs and ViTs**, not for diffusion / flow-matching heads. The action expert in π0.5 and similar models requires custom hooks because TransformerLens v3 does not natively understand denoising timesteps.
- **Modality-gap analysis is contested**: Liang et al. (NeurIPS 2022) treat it as a bug; Eslami & de Melo (2024) argue it is intrinsic to contrastive loss and that "closing the gap" improves clustering but not retrieval. Don't expect modality-gap closure to be a universal good.
- **Probe accuracy as a training signal can be gamed**: a probe is only as good as its target — high gripper-state probe accuracy doesn't imply good manipulation. The probe suite should always include *task-relevant* probes (success prediction, EE-pose regression), not just easy auxiliary tasks.
- **No published peer-reviewed work** yet stress-tests the proposed toolkit recommendation end-to-end; this report's Stage-1/2/3 plan is a synthesis from disparate cases, not a benchmarked workflow.
- **arXiv IDs 2603.19183 (Dr. VLA), 2604.13645 (Lei et al.), and 2510.01711 (RS-CL) are recent (late 2025 / 2026) preprints** that have not all completed peer review at the time of writing; results should be treated as preprint-grade rather than archival.
