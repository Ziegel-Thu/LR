# DR-016: Geometric Signatures of "Understanding" in Representation Space

## Executive Answer

Across the models that claim some form of "understanding"—V-JEPA 2, I-JEPA, DreamerV3, Genie, OthelloGPT, Coconut, Huginn, CLIP/ImageBind, and various LLM-as-world-model systems—there is **strong but indirect evidence that they share a common geometric fingerprint**: representations that are (i) **drastically lower-dimensional than the ambient hidden size** (intrinsic dimension typically O(10), against O(10³) units), (ii) shaped along depth into a **"hunchback" or expansion–contraction profile** with a mid/late-layer **semantic plateau / ID valley**, and (iii) populated by **linearly probable concept directions** whose accuracy on the labelled concept scales with task competence. However, **almost none of these models has been measured systematically with the same geometric toolkit**; the evidence is fragmented across protein language models (Valeriani 2023), CNN image classifiers (Ansuini 2019), GPT‑style LMs (Cheng 2023/24, Ferrara 2024), AlexNet manifolds (Cohen & Chung 2020) and OthelloGPT (Li 2022, Nanda 2023). Whether the semantic plateau and high manifold capacity are *the* signature of understanding, or merely of any well-trained network on a structured task, remains an **open empirical question and a high-value experimental opportunity**.

## Task 1 — Models that claim "understanding"

The table below summarises the principal models, what they claim to understand, how the claim is evaluated, what is known about their representation geometry, and their open-source status.

**V-JEPA 2 (Meta, 2025).** Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture trained on >1M hours of video plus an action-conditioned 300M-parameter head fine-tuned on ~62h of Droid robot data. Claim: *physical-world understanding, prediction, and zero-shot robot planning*. Evaluation: motion-understanding probes (77.3% top-1 on Something-Something v2), action anticipation (39.7 R@5 Epic-Kitchens-100), and three new physical-reasoning benchmarks released alongside the model where V-JEPA 2 lags humans (85–95% accuracy) by a sizable margin. Representation-geometry analysis: **none published**; only probe-based linear-readout evaluations exist. Open-source: weights released by Meta. Encoder ~1.2B parameters.

**I-JEPA (Meta, 2023).** Image-domain JEPA predicting masked patch representations from context patches. Claim: visual scene understanding via abstract (non-pixel) targets. Evaluated through linear probing on ImageNet, low-shot classification, and depth/segmentation transfer. No published ID profile or manifold-capacity analysis. Open-source.

**DreamerV3 (Hafner et al., 2023).** Recurrent State-Space Model that learns a latent world model and trains an actor–critic by imagining rollouts in latent space. Claim: domain-general "world dynamics" understanding spanning continuous control, Atari, and Minecraft diamond collection with fixed hyperparameters. Evaluation: task return curves; latent reconstruction loss; behavioural transfer. Geometry analysis: essentially none in the original paper; downstream work (MuDreamer, NE-Dreamer, R2-Dreamer) inspects latent reconstructability but not ID/manifold structure. Open-source. Scale: tens of millions of parameters to ~200M depending on variant.

**Genie 1/2/3 (DeepMind, 2024–2025).** Foundation video world model (~11B for Genie 1) with a learned discrete latent-action codebook trained unsupervised on >200K hours of internet gameplay. Claim: emergent *action semantics* — controllable latent actions despite no action labels. Evaluation: video PSNR, FID, and "controllability" via learned action codes. No public ID/manifold analysis. Not fully open-source (model not released; only the Genie 1 paper is). Scale: 11B (Genie 1), Genie 3 unstated.

**OthelloGPT (Li et al., 2023; Nanda 2023; Hazineh 2023).** Decoder-only Transformer trained only on move sequences. Claim: emergent "world model" of the 8×8 board. Evaluation: probes for the board state. Geometry: **this is the gold standard for understanding-geometry analysis**. Li et al. originally claimed only non-linear probing recovered the board; Nanda then showed that re-parameterising as MINE/YOURS/EMPTY recovers the same world model **linearly** (linear probes from ~90% at layer 1 up to ~99.6% mid-stack vs 75% for BLACK/WHITE/EMPTY). Causal-intervention experiments confirm that this linear direction is functionally used. Hazineh's TPR probes further show the linear directions factor into "square × colour" tensor-product structure, with the probe weights themselves exhibiting board-geometric structure. Open-source.

**Coconut (Hao et al., Meta, 2024).** Modifies GPT-2 / Llama to feed the last hidden state back as the next input embedding ("continuous thought") instead of decoding a token. Claim: latent-space reasoning that can encode multiple alternative next steps (BFS-like). Evaluation: GSM8K, ProsQA, ProntoQA accuracy; emergent-pattern analysis via decoding latents back into tokens. Geometry analysis: a 2025 follow-up ("Do Latent Tokens Think? A Causal and Adversarial Analysis", arXiv:2512.21711) uses orthogonal perturbations and cross-input swapping of latent tokens and finds they sometimes act as causally meaningful reasoning carriers but exhibit shortcut behaviour OOD. No ID or manifold-capacity profile reported.

**Huginn-3.5B (Geiping et al., 2025).** Depth-recurrent Transformer with a core block unrolled many times at inference. Claim: latent chain-of-thought reasoning. Evaluation: math/code/commonsense accuracy at increasing recurrence depth, plus logit-lens / coda-lens probes (Lu et al. 2025, "Latent Chain-of-Thought? Decoding the Depth-Recurrent Transformer"), and the LRM/LTO experiments of Du et al. (2025) showing correct vs incorrect trajectories are linearly discriminable. Empirically, the authors report three latent "thinking modes" — convergence, orbits, and sliders — which is an explicit geometric claim that, to date, has not been quantified with ID or manifold metrics. Open-source: weights and ~350 checkpoints available on Hugging Face.

**CLIP / ImageBind (OpenAI, Meta).** Contrastive image–text (and multi-modal) encoders. Claim: cross-modal *semantic* understanding. Evaluation: zero-shot ImageNet, retrieval, robustness. Geometry analysis is **richer here than elsewhere**: papers document a persistent **modality gap** between image and text cones (Liang et al. 2022), a "double-ellipsoid" structure (Zhang et al. 2024, arXiv:2411.14517), and theoretical results (arXiv:2505.12473) showing that multi-modal contrastive learning **adapts to the intrinsic dimension of the shared data**, with ID of in-sample CLIP representations ≤10 regardless of nominal output dimension. Open-source.

**LLM "world model" claims.** Beyond OthelloGPT, work has shown linear probes that recover space and time (Gurnee & Tegmark 2024), board state in chess transformers, truthfulness directions (Burns et al.), and Latent Semantic Manifolds (arXiv:2603.22301) which formally connects ID/curvature to next-token loss. These are commonly cited as evidence of "world models in LLMs." The empirical signature is again *low-ID, linearly probable* concept directions.

In sum, every model on this list except CLIP and Huginn has only sparse or anecdotal geometric characterisation; for V-JEPA 2, I-JEPA, DreamerV3, and Genie the representation geometry has effectively not been measured with the modern ID / manifold-capacity / topology toolkit at all.

## Task 2 — Existing "understanding × geometry" literature

The relevant literature converges on three connected lines of evidence.

**Intrinsic-dimension profile predicts performance.** Ansuini et al. (NeurIPS 2019) measured ID with the TwoNN estimator across many CNNs (VGG, ResNet, AlexNet, DenseNet, and others) trained on ImageNet. They found (i) the ID of the last hidden layer **predicts test accuracy** across architectures, (ii) ID across layers shows a "hunchback" — short rise, broad fall, low final value (peak 100–120 at relative depth 0.2–0.4 for VGG/ResNet) — and crucially (iii) the hunchback is **absent in untrained networks** and in networks trained with randomised labels. PCA-based linear-dim estimates do *not* distinguish trained from untrained networks; only non-linear ID does. This anchors the empirical claim that the hunchback shape itself is a fingerprint of learning structured representations, not merely of having layers.

**Semantic plateaus in large transformers.** Valeriani, Doimo, Cuturello, Laio, Ansuini & Cazzaniga (2023, "The geometry of hidden representations of large transformer models") extended Ansuini's profile to protein language models (ESM family), image transformers (ViT/BEiT), and BERT-style text models. They report a three-phase pattern: an initial **expansion** (ID grows), a **contraction** to a low-ID **semantic plateau** in middle/late layers, and a final task-specific re-organisation. Layers in the ID minimum yield the best downstream linear-probe performance for tasks like remote-homology detection — i.e., the ID valley *is* the semantically rich zone. They argue this is "a general computational strategy."

**The pattern recurs in LMs and beyond.** Cheng et al. (2023, 2024) measured layerwise ID on GPT-2, Pythia, and Llama-family models using GRIDE/TwoNN and observed the same expansion–contraction with ID ≈ O(10) at the plateau. Ferrara et al. (2024, "Latent Semantic Manifolds in LLMs", arXiv:2603.22301) report a correlation between ID and next-token loss. Ruppik et al. (2025, arXiv:2506.01034, "Less is More: Local Intrinsic Dimensions of Contextual Language Models") show that local ID predicts fine-tuning quality and generalisation. ACL-Long 2025 ("Geometric Signatures of Compositionality") argues that **compositional capability** correlates with low manifold ID. Ruppik et al. (2025, "Towards Understanding the Shape of Representations in PLMs", arXiv:2509.24895) corroborate the universal expansion–contraction shape and link it to "general high-abstraction" vs "specific semantically rich" regimes.

**Manifold capacity links geometry to classification competence.** Chung, Cohen, Lee & Sompolinsky (Nat. Commun. 2020) extended Gardner-style perceptron capacity to manifolds of category exemplars. Their AlexNet analysis is the cleanest controlled study available: capacity, manifold radius, and manifold dimension all improve along the trained hierarchy, **but stay flat in randomly initialised networks** and in label-shuffled controls. Mignacco, Chou & Chung (PRX/PNAS-Nexus 2025) generalised this to context-dependent manifold capacity and showed it captures representational reformatting in deep nets that simpler ID metrics miss. A 2025 paper ("Feature Learning beyond the Lazy-Rich Dichotomy", arXiv:2503.18114) uses manifold capacity to detect *learning stages* during training, showing that representation-untangling is monotonic only under rich-regime feature learning.

**Linear probability of physical/causal quantities.** OthelloGPT (above) is the headline example of a linearly recoverable world variable. For physical world models, recent work (Mencattini et al., "The Observer Effect in World Models: Invasive Adaptation Corrupts Latent Physics", arXiv:2602.12218) proposes *PhyIP*, a non-invasive linear probe of frozen SSL representations for time-independent conserved quantities, distilled into symbolic formulas. This work explicitly argues that linear time-independent probes are the right instrument for measuring whether a world model has internalised physical laws — but it has not yet been applied to V-JEPA 2 or DreamerV3 publicly.

**Geometry vs memorisation.** Ansuini et al.'s randomised-label control demonstrates that memorising networks fail to develop the low-ID terminal layer that predicts test accuracy. Tulchinskii et al. (2023) use ID to *distinguish AI-generated from human text* — a separate but related sign that meaningful generation lives on lower-ID manifolds than spurious generation.

## Task 3 — Is Valeriani's semantic plateau universal?

**Where it has been observed.** The expansion–contraction profile with a mid-/late-layer ID minimum has now been reported in: protein language models (Valeriani 2023; Ruppik 2025); BERT and GPT-2-class text LMs (Cheng 2023/24; Valeriani 2023; Ferrara 2024); image transformers and ViTs (Valeriani 2023); CNN image classifiers, dating back to Ansuini 2019; AI-generated-text detectors (Tulchinskii 2023); and contextual LMs at the level of *local* ID (Ruppik 2025). The Latent Semantic Manifolds paper (arXiv:2603.22301) explicitly elevates the hunchback from a pLM curiosity to a candidate universal property of competent transformer-based generation, and notes that the literature is "exclusively empirical: it describes what geometric properties representations exhibit, but does not explain why."

**Where it has not been measured (clear experimental gaps).** None of the following has, to public knowledge, a published TwoNN/GRIDE layerwise ID profile:
- **V-JEPA 2** encoder or its action-conditioned predictor — despite explicit physical-understanding claims.
- **I-JEPA** at scale.
- **DreamerV3** RSSM latents (z_t, h_t) — though the entire system is built around a learned latent.
- **Genie** spatiotemporal tokenizer or latent action codebook.
- **Coconut** continuous thoughts across reasoning steps (only logit-lens-style probes exist).
- **Huginn-3.5B** across recurrent unrollings — although the qualitative "orbits/sliders/convergence" report suggests highly non-trivial geometry that an ID/curvature profile could quantify.
- **ImageBind** at the joint-embedding level (CLIP has partial coverage via the modality-gap and intrinsic-dimension-adaptation papers).

These are precisely the models making the strongest "understanding" claims. The semantic-plateau hypothesis is therefore *plausible but unverified* for the world-model and latent-reasoning frontier. Whether the plateau also appears in models trained with non-reconstructive objectives (JEPA-style targets, DreamerV3-without-decoder variants like MuDreamer, NE-Dreamer) is a particularly interesting open question because their representations are trained to be predictively sufficient rather than reconstructively faithful — exactly the regime where a "more meaningful" semantic plateau might be expected.

## Task 4 — Controlled "understanding vs not-understanding" studies

**Trained vs random init (same architecture).** Two landmark studies make this comparison cleanly. (i) Ansuini et al. 2019 show the ID hunchback collapses in untrained networks — there, each layer behaves like an orthogonal transformation and ID stays roughly flat from input. (ii) Cohen, Chung, Lee & Sompolinsky (Nat. Commun. 2020) compute manifold capacity, manifold dimension, manifold radius, and centre-correlation along AlexNet for fully trained, randomly initialised, and label-shuffled conditions. Capacity and geometry monotonically improve through the trained hierarchy but stay essentially constant for the random network. Shuffling object membership eliminates almost all residual structure, confirming that the geometry tracks *meaningful* category structure rather than architecture alone.

**Well-trained vs undertrained (training dynamics).** Recent work (arXiv:2503.18114, 2025) measures manifold capacity throughout training and discovers discrete learning *stages*: an initial lazy phase (manifold geometry barely changes), followed by rich phases that aggressively untangle. The trajectory differs by hyperparameters in ways finer than the lazy/rich dichotomy. Mignacco et al. (PRX/PNAS-Nexus 2025) show context-dependent manifold capacity captures intermediate reformattings inaccessible to mean-only ID. Cohen 2025 ("How does training shape the Riemannian geometry of neural network representations?", arXiv:2301.11375) explicitly studies the metric tensor of trained vs untrained feature maps and finds that training enhances *local input discriminability* — a quantity related to but distinct from ID.

**Learned X vs didn't learn X (objective ablations).** The retinal-waves SimCLR study (Cyr & Sengupta 2023, PMC10723543) shows networks pre-trained even on biologically realistic noise produce more separable manifolds than random nets, indicating that *some* meaningful unsupervised signal is enough. Conversely, Ansuini's label-shuffled networks possess the same architectures and learn to fit the data, yet fail to develop the low terminal ID — meaningful generalisation, not training, is what shapes the low-ID terminal manifold.

**Memorisation vs understanding.** The combination of Zhang et al. (2017, "rethinking generalisation") and Ansuini 2019 implies an operational distinction: memorising-only networks reach high training accuracy without the ID drop, while generalising networks do drop ID. The drop magnitude correlates with test accuracy across architectures.

## Task 5 — Most promising experiments

Based on the above, the gap between rich understanding claims and the comparative absence of cross-model geometric measurement points to a *small set of high-leverage experiments*. None of these requires new theory; all use established tools.

**1. Cross-model ID-profile atlas.** Compute layerwise TwoNN and GRIDE ID for V-JEPA 2 (encoder + AC head), I-JEPA, DreamerV3 RSSM latents (and MuDreamer/NE-Dreamer for the decoder-free contrast), Genie's tokenizer and dynamics model, OthelloGPT, Coconut continuous-thought states, Huginn-3.5B across recurrence steps, and CLIP/ImageBind, all on a matched stimulus set (a curated set of physical-interaction videos plus text captions for cross-modal models). *Predictions:* (a) all show ID ≈ O(10) despite hidden sizes O(10³); (b) all show a hunchback with a semantic plateau in middle layers; (c) the depth of the ID minimum (relative to ambient) correlates positively with reported task performance; (d) JEPA-style predictive objectives produce a *deeper, longer* plateau than reconstruction-style objectives (predicted because they discard reconstruction-only nuisance variance). A negative finding here — e.g., DreamerV3 lacking the hunchback — would be highly informative because it would suggest that reinforcement-learning world models pack information differently from supervised/contrastive understanders.

**2. Manifold-capacity ladder of understanding tasks.** Apply Chung-style replica manifold capacity (and Cohen 2020 manifold dimension/radius) to neural-manifold definitions matched to each model's claimed understanding: object-identity manifolds for V-JEPA 2 (CO3D), board-state manifolds for OthelloGPT, scene-physics-state manifolds for DreamerV3 (DM Control physics variations), and "reasoning-step" manifolds for Coconut/Huginn (groups of latent thoughts that reach the same correct answer). *Predictions:* capacity improves along depth in trained models, is flat or noisy in random/undertrained controls, and the layer of peak capacity coincides with the ID minimum. Coconut and Huginn are particularly attractive because Huginn provides ~350 intermediate checkpoints, enabling a continuous capacity-vs-training curve.

**3. Linear-probe physics test on V-JEPA 2 / DreamerV3.** Following the PhyIP framework (arXiv:2602.12218), apply *time-independent linear* probes for conserved quantities — momentum, energy, position, velocity, contact — on frozen V-JEPA 2 and DreamerV3 latents, with strict Hewitt-Liang control probes to rule out probe expressivity. A positive result (probe accuracy ≥ control by a fixed margin, OOD robust) would constitute concrete evidence that the model *linearly represents* physical quantities; a negative result would directly challenge the "physical understanding" framing and recast V-JEPA 2's success as appearance-based.

**4. Trained vs ablated "understanding axis."** Replicate the Ansuini 2019 randomised-label control in modern world-model settings: for example, train DreamerV3 with shuffled action–state pairs (destroying dynamics structure) and compare to standard training. Likewise, train Coconut with shuffled chain-of-thought supervision. *Prediction:* shuffled-objective controls retain training-loss curves comparable to standard but fail to develop the low-ID semantic plateau and the high manifold capacity in late layers. This would establish the plateau as a signature of *meaningful* training rather than architecture.

**5. Persistent-homology and curvature on Huginn recurrence orbits.** The Huginn paper qualitatively reports "orbits, sliders, convergence" in latent reasoning. Persistent homology (e.g., topological data analysis á la Tulchinskii 2023) and Riemannian curvature (Cohen 2023) of the trajectory through recurrent depth would quantify these. *Prediction:* successful reasoning trajectories should have lower topological complexity (fewer 1-cycles surviving above a small ε) and lower local curvature than unsuccessful ones; this would also provide a non-invasive test-time stopping rule.

**6. Cross-modal ID alignment.** For CLIP and ImageBind, use Intrinsic Dimension Correlation (arXiv:2406.15812) to measure non-linear alignment of cross-modal subspaces, and link this to the modality-gap and double-ellipsoid findings. *Prediction:* better-aligned models (with smaller modality gap after gap-mitigation interventions) should have higher IDcor, lower terminal ID, and better zero-shot retrieval — establishing IDcor as a candidate scalar metric of "cross-modal understanding."

**Appropriate tools.** TwoNN and GRIDE for global ID; local intrinsic dimension (Ruppik 2025) when token-level granularity matters; manifold capacity, manifold dimension, manifold radius, and centre-correlation from the Chung/Cohen replica framework; persistent homology (Vietoris–Rips diagrams) for topological complexity; the Riemannian metric pull-back (arXiv:2301.11375) for local curvature/discriminability; linear and TPR probes with Hewitt-Liang controls for concept-direction recovery; and IDcor for non-linear cross-representation comparisons.

**Interpreting positive and negative results.** A positive cross-model finding — a shared hunchback + semantic plateau + high manifold capacity + linearly probable task-relevant concepts — would consolidate "understanding" into a measurable geometric *operational definition* and would let one rank models by it without task-specific benchmarks. A negative finding (e.g., DreamerV3 latents fail to show the plateau even though they support successful planning) would be **at least as interesting**: it would mean either (a) "understanding" is not a single geometric construct but a small family of distinct ones (predictive sufficiency, semantic compression, causal-variable linearity) that come apart across paradigms, or (b) some of the public claims of understanding are overstated. Either outcome would substantially clarify a currently ambiguous discourse.

## Caveats

Several papers cited above are very recent (2025) preprints and use the language of "we hypothesize" or "we expect" — including the Huginn latent-pattern taxonomy, the "Observer Effect" PhyIP framework, and the lazy-rich manifold-capacity work. The claim that the ID hunchback is a *universal* signature is empirically supported for protein LMs, image CNNs, image transformers, and text LMs, but has not been tested on JEPA-style, world-model, or latent-reasoning architectures — that is the central gap this research direction would close. Marketing-style claims of "understanding" (especially in V-JEPA 2 and Genie press materials) should be treated as hypotheses about representation structure that the geometric program proposed here can directly test.
